# Circular Motion

Calculate the **centripetal acceleration** of a person standing on the Earth's equator.

Given:

- Earth's radius: $R = 6378 \text{ km} = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$
- Earth completes one rotation in $T = 24 \text{ h} = 86400 \text{ s}$

---

# 1. Angular Velocity of the Earth

Angular velocity is

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

Substitute the value of $T$:

$$
\omega = \frac{2\pi}{86400}
$$

$$
\omega \approx 7.27 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}
$$

---

# 2. Centripetal Acceleration Formula

Centripetal acceleration is

$$
a_c = \omega^2 R
$$

---

# 3. Substitute Values

First compute $\omega^2$:

$$
\omega^2 = (7.27 \times 10^{-5})^2
$$

$$
\omega^2 \approx 5.29 \times 10^{-9}
$$

Now multiply by $R$:

$$
a_c = (5.29 \times 10^{-9})(6.378 \times 10^6)
$$

$$
a_c \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

---

# Final Result

The centripetal acceleration of a person standing on the Earth's equator is approximately

$$
a_c \approx 0.034 \text{ m/s}^2
$$

This is **much smaller than gravitational acceleration** ($g \approx 9.81\,\text{m/s}^2$).
