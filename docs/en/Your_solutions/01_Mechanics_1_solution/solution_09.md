# Momentum Comparison

Momentum is defined as

$$
p = mv
$$

where:

- $p$ = momentum  
- $m$ = mass  
- $v$ = velocity  

Mass must be converted to **kilograms**.

---

# 1. Momentum of the Fly

Mass of fly:

$$
2\,\text{g} = 0.002\,\text{kg}
$$

Velocity:

$$
v = 10\,\text{m/s}
$$

Momentum:

$$
p = mv
$$

$$
p = 0.002 \times 10
$$

$$
p = 0.02\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

---

# 2. Momentum of the Tennis Ball

Mass of tennis ball:

$$
60\,\text{g} = 0.06\,\text{kg}
$$

Velocity:

$$
v = 1\,\text{m/s}
$$

Momentum:

$$
p = mv
$$

$$
p = 0.06 \times 1
$$

$$
p = 0.06\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}
$$

---

# 3. Comparison

| Object | Momentum |
|---|---|
| Fly | $0.02\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$ |
| Tennis Ball | $0.06\,\text{kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$ |

---

# Final Result

The **tennis ball has greater momentum**.

$$
0.06 > 0.02
$$

The tennis ball has **three times more momentum** than the fly.
