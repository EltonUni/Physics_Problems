# Relative Velocity

A river flows **east at $2\,\text{m/s}$**.  
A boat moves at **$5\,\text{m/s}$** relative to the water and wants to go **directly north** across the river.

River width: **$200\,\text{m}$**

We need to determine:

1. The direction (angle) the boat should head.
2. The time needed to cross the river.

---

# 1. Velocity Components

Let:

- $v_b = 5\,\text{m/s}$ (boat speed relative to water)  
- $v_r = 2\,\text{m/s}$ (river current east)

To go **directly north**, the boat must cancel the river’s eastward velocity.

If the boat heads **$\theta$ west of north**, its westward velocity component must equal the river current.

---

# 2. Cancel the River Current

The west component of the boat’s velocity is:

$$
v_b \sin\theta
$$

Set this equal to the river speed:

$$
5\sin\theta = 2
$$

$$
\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5}
$$

$$
\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)
$$

$$
\theta \approx 23.6^\circ
$$

Therefore, the boat must head **$23.6^\circ$ west of north**.

---

# 3. Effective Northward Velocity

The northward component of the boat’s velocity is:

$$
v_n = v_b \cos\theta
$$

$$
v_n = 5\cos(23.6^\circ)
$$

$$
v_n \approx 5(0.917)
$$

$$
v_n \approx 4.58\,\text{m/s}
$$

---

# 4. Time to Cross the River

Time is calculated by

$$
t = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{velocity}}
$$

$$
t = \frac{200}{4.58}
$$

$$
t \approx 43.7\,\text{s}
$$

---

# Final Results

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---|
| Heading Direction | **$23.6^\circ$ west of north** |
| Crossing Time | **$43.7\,\text{s}$** |
