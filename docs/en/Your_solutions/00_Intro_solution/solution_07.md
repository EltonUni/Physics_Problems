## Problem

A bicycle is $10$ meters from a wall and moves toward it at a constant speed of $1\text{ m/s}$.  
A fly starts from the bicycle's front wheel and flies toward the wall at $2\text{ m/s}$.  

When the fly reaches the wall, it instantly turns back and flies to the bicycle, repeating this process until the bicycle reaches the wall.

Find the **total distance the fly travels** before being crushed.

---

## Step 1 — Time for the bicycle to reach the wall

We first calculate how long it takes for the bicycle to hit the wall.

The formula for time is:

$$
t = \frac{d}{v}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
t = \frac{10}{1}
$$

$$
t = 10
$$

So the bicycle reaches the wall after

$$
t = 10 \text{ seconds}
$$

---

## Step 2 — Fly speed

The fly moves continuously during this entire time at a constant speed of

$$
v_f = 2\text{ m/s}
$$

Since the fly keeps flying until the bicycle reaches the wall, the total flying time is also

$$
t = 10 \text{ s}
$$

---

## Step 3 — Distance traveled by the fly

Use the distance formula:

$$
d = vt
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
d = 2 \times 10
$$

$$
d = 20
$$

---

## Final Answer

The total distance traveled by the fly is

$$
\boxed{20\text{ meters}}
$$
